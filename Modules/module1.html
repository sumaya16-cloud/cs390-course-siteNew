Module 1: Foundations & Mathematical Readiness

Module Overview

This module introduces course expectations, course tools, and the mathematical foundations required for theoretical computer science. Students review sets, relations, functions, notation, proof techniques, strings, alphabets, and formal languages that support all later topics in automata theory.

Success in theoretical computer science requires careful reading, precise notation, and logical reasoning. This module prepares students for later topics including finite automata, regular expressions, grammars, pushdown automata, and Turing machines.

Module Learning Objectives

After completing this module, students should be able to:

  • Explain key discrete mathematics concepts including sets, relations, and functions.
  • Interpret and construct formal definitions using mathematical notation.
  • Describe strings, alphabets, and formal languages.
  • Apply introductory proof-writing and logical reasoning techniques.
  • Use mathematical notation commonly found in automata theory.

Topics Covered

  • Sets
  • Relations
  • Functions
  • Mathematical notation
  • Methods of proof
  • Strings and alphabets
  • Formal languages
  • Reading and writing mathematical expressions
