Module 1: Foundations & Mathematical Readiness

Module Overview

This module introduces course expectations, tools, and the mathematical foundations required for theoretical computer science. Students review sets, relations, functions, notation, strings, and formal languages, which support all later topics in automata theory.

Module Learning Objectives

  • Explain key discrete mathematics concepts including sets, relations, and functions.
  • Interpret and construct formal definitions using mathematical notation.
  • Describe the concept of a formal language and its role in computation.

Topics

  • Sets, relations, and functions
  • Mathematical notation and proof readiness
  • Strings, alphabets, and formal languages

Required Readings & Resources

  • Hopcroft, Motwani, and Ullman, Chapter 1
  • Dr. Zeil’s notes on strings and languages
  • Mathematics review self-assessment

Assignments

  • Quiz 1: Foundations & Math Review
  • Self-Assessment: Mathematics Review
